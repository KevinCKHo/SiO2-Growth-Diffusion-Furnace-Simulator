Learning Notes

Dry vs Wet: Dry gives denser, lower-interface-state oxide but 5–10× slower. Wet uses H₂O vapor for thick field oxide.

Deal-Grove: Parabolic (B) dominates thick oxide; linear (B/A) dominates thin oxide regime.

Diffusion: Arrhenius D = D₀·exp(−Eₐ/kT). Junction depth ∝ √(Dt).

Laminar Flow: Re < 2300. Parabolic profile ensures uniform gas delivery to wafer surface.
